Hi! I am Maddie Goldberg


Developmental Cognitive Neuroscience PhD student at the University of Oregon


My work primarily focuses on reward-related neuroendocrine relationships across adoelscence, particularly how adrenal androgens and gonadal steroids partly affect reward-related brain function and drive decision-making. I recieved my B.S. in Psychology (with Honors) and additional Applied Statitstics and Sociology minors at the University of Pittsburgh. I also recieved a competative 2-year post-baccalaureate IRTA Fellowship from the National Institutes of Mental Health within the Sections on Behavioral Endocrinology and Integrative Neuroscience.


Outside of work I love to swim, bike, run (no triatholons yet...), and read!
